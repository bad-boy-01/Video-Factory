Chapter 1: The Station

Detective Maya adjusted the collar of her trench coat, shielding her face from the driving rain. The abandoned train station loomed ahead, its massive steel arches casting long, skeletal shadows in the dying light of the sunset. The power had been cut for days, leaving the terminal in pitch blackness save for the occasional flash of lightning.

She clicked her flashlight on. The harsh beam sliced through the darkness, illuminating dust motes dancing in the damp air.

"Ethan?" she called out. Her voice echoed off the cracked tile floor, small and fragile in the cavernous space.

"I'm here," a voice replied. Ethan stepped out from behind a rusted ticket kiosk. He looked exhausted, his clothes soaked through.

"You shouldn't have come," Ethan said, his voice tense. 
"You didn't leave me much of a choice," Maya shot back, the argument flaring up instantly between them. 

Suddenly, a metallic clang echoed from the platform above. 

Maya's flashlight swept up, catching the briefest glimpse of a figure moving fast along the catwalk. 

"Run!" Ethan yelled. 

They sprinted toward the old maintenance tunnels as footsteps thundered down the metal stairs behind them. Maya didn't look back, she just ran, the beam of her flashlight bouncing wildly off the concrete walls as they made their escape.
